Adjectives

How French Adjectives Work

1.) The first main difference between French adjectives and English adjectives is that in French, the adjective always comes AFTER the subject or noun. For example, in English I would say "My pink dress" but in French, I would say "Ma robe rose." The only exception to this rule is B.A.G.S adjectives, which you can read more about in the first section.

2.) The second main difference between French and English adjectives is that French adjectives are affected by whether the subject is male, female, or in the plural form. While in English phrases like "He is tall" and "She is tall" will have the same adjective, in French it would be "Il est grand" and "Elle est grande." Most adjectives will follow the general rules below but there are still many who will follow an irregular pattern. Therefore, it is imperitive to learn all the appropriate forms when learning about an adjective.

Unlike English, most French adjectives come after the subject they are refering to. However, there is one excpetion: B.A.G.S adjectives. B.A.G.S is an acronym that stands for Beauty, Age, Goodness, and Size. Many adjectives that fall into these categories will precede the noun, just like how adjectives work in English. HOWEVER, not all adjectives that fit the category precede the noun. If the adjective is not in this list, very likely it is not actually a B.A.G.S adjective!
