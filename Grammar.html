The most common type of verb in the French language is the -er verb. Left in this form, the verb acts as a infinitive (to _____). While some verbs will follow a special rule, most -er verbs will be conjugated as followed. A common exception, however, for -er verbs is in the case of the verb ending in -ger. In this case, the nous form must use -eons as its conjugation. A present tense verb has three meanings in French: I am doing ________, I _________, I do _________.

Il/Elle -it

Nous -issons

Vous -issez

Ils/Elles -issent

Another common type of verb is the -IR verbs. The majority of verbs in this class are conjegated as follows and have the same meaning as the -ER verbs. Verbs ending in -LLIR, -FRIR, and -VRIR will be conjegated as -ER verbs. There is also a large class of verbs with the endng -ENIR that are conjegated differently, as well as specific irregular -IR verbs.

The last common class of verbs is the -RE verbs. Again, verbs of this type hold the same meaning and are for the majority conjugated as follows. -RE verbs have many types of irregular patterns, which can be seen on the Verbs page.

Je ai -drop the ER, add é

Tu as -drop the ER, add é

Il/Elle a -drop the ER, add é

Nous avons -drop the ER, add é

Vous avez -drop the ER, add é

Ils/Elles ont -drop the ER, add é

In the past tense, French -ER verbs take the form avoir + the past participle of the verb. In essence, you are conjugating avoir in terms of the pronoun, and then adding the verb form with a é instead of the -ER. The past tense can mean three things: I _____, I did _____, I have _____.

Je ai -drop the IR, add is

Tu as -drop the IR, add is

Il/Elle a -drop the IR, add it

Nous avons -drop the IR, add issons

Vous avez -drop the IR, add issez

Ils/Elles ont -drop the IR, add issent

Like the -ER verbs, -IR verbs take the form avoir + their past participle. Again, verbs ending in -LLIR, -FRIR, and -VRIR will be conjugated like -ER verbs, except this time, it will be the past tense of the -ER verbs.

Je ai drop the -RE, add s

Tu as drop the -RE, add s

Il/Elle a drop the -RE

Nous ont drop the -RE, add ons

Vous avez drop the -RE, add ez

Ils/Elles avez drop the -RE, add ent

-RE verbs will follow the same avoir + participle formation as before.
