Present Tense -ER Verb Conjugation

Je -e

Tu -es

Il/Elle -e

Nous -ons

Vous -ez

Ils/Elles -ent

The most common type of verb in the French language is the -er verb. Left in this form, the verb acts as a infinitive (to _____). While some verbs will follow a special rule, most -er verbs will be conjugated as followed. A common exception, however, for -er verbs is in the case of the verb ending in -ger. In this case, the nous form must use -eons as its conjugation. A present tense verb has three meanings in French: I am doing ________, I _________, I do _________.

Present Tense -IR Verb Conjugation

Je -is

Tu -is

Il/Elle -it

Nous -issons

Vous -issez

Ils/Elles -issent

Another common type of verb is the -IR verbs. The majority of verbs in this class are conjegated as follows and have the same meaning as the -ER verbs. Verbs ending in -LLIR, -FRIR, and -VRIR will be conjegated as -ER verbs. There is also a large class of verbs with the endng -ENIR that are conjegated differently, as well as specific irregular -IR verbs.

Present Tense -RE Verb Conjugation

Il/Elle Drop the -RE

Nous -ons

Vous -ez

Ils/Elles -ent

The last common class of verbs is the -RE verbs. Again, verbs of this type hold the same meaning and are for the majority conjugated as follows. -RE verbs have many types of irregular patterns, which will be seen later on.
